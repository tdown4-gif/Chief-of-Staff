# Substitutes

## Current User Workarounds

Users already solve parts of this problem with a patchwork of tools and habits:

- Notes apps.
- Messaging themselves.
- Email drafts.
- Spreadsheets.
- Calendar descriptions.
- Task managers.
- Bookmark managers.
- Desktop folders.
- CRM-lite documents.
- Browser tabs.
- AI chat history.

## Why Substitutes Are Hard To Replace

Substitutes are familiar, flexible, and already embedded in daily workflows. Users may not feel immediate pain because their current system is messy but survivable.

Chief of Staff must therefore provide immediate value without requiring a migration project.

## What Substitutes Fail To Do

- They do not organize automatically.
- They do not build a durable memory graph.
- They do not connect people, companies, ideas, projects, tasks, events, documents, skills, opportunities, trips, preferences, and subscriptions.
- They do not explain why something was remembered.
- They do not reliably suggest grounded next actions.
- They do not become meaningfully more useful over time without manual upkeep.

## V1 Substitution Strategy

The product should not ask users to replace every workflow immediately. It should become the place where important loose context goes first.

The wedge is capture:

- Faster than opening a structured tool.
- Smarter than a notes app.
- More durable than a chat message to yourself.
- More proactive than a folder.

## Anti-Substitutes

Chief of Staff should explicitly avoid becoming:

- Full CRM
- Full calendar app
- Travel booking
- Password manager
- Expense management suite
- Enterprise collaboration
- Browser extension
- Native mobile app
- Complex dashboards
