# User Personas

## Founder

### Context

Founders carry context across investors, customers, hires, partners, ideas, product decisions, and opportunities.

### Needs

- Capture scattered thoughts quickly.
- Remember people and companies.
- Track opportunities without a full CRM.
- Turn ideas into projects.
- Prepare for meetings and follow-ups.

### Risk

If the product becomes heavy, the founder will default back to notes and chat.

## Operator

### Context

Operators manage projects, people, documents, decisions, vendors, and recurring obligations.

### Needs

- Link documents, tasks, people, and events.
- Remember decisions.
- Surface stale follow-ups.
- Track subscriptions and recurring commitments.

### Risk

If suggestions are noisy, the product becomes another inbox.

## Consultant

### Context

Consultants move across clients, research, deliverables, relationships, and opportunities.

### Needs

- Organize client context.
- Track companies and people.
- Maintain reusable research.
- Detect follow-up and expansion opportunities.

### Risk

The product may drift toward CRM if boundaries are not clear.

## Researcher Or Builder

### Context

Researchers and builders accumulate ideas, documents, skills, experiments, grants, and market signals.

### Needs

- Connect ideas to projects.
- Track open questions.
- Build a skills graph.
- Detect opportunities, including SBIR tracking.
- Generate project drafts from captured ideas.

### Risk

The product may feel like a knowledge base unless it suggests concrete actions.

## Job Seeker

### Context

Job seekers manage companies, people, applications, resumes, skills, projects, and follow-ups.

### Needs

- Track companies and opportunities.
- Remember relationship context.
- Suggest resume updates.
- Build skills graph.
- Create follow-up reminders.

### Risk

The product must avoid becoming a job board or full CRM.
